# Haenyeo Culture Walk: Coastal Path and Community Museum

## Summary

This experience pairs a walk along the coastal path near a haenyeo village with a visit to a community museum documenting the history and practice of haenyeo diving. The walk typically follows the shoreline where haenyeo still dive, passing bulteok (traditional gathering shelters) and diving access points, before continuing to exhibits that explain diving technique, community structure, and the tradition's UNESCO recognition.

## Why It Matters

Haenyeo culture (see `STORY-0001`) is easy to romanticize from a distance and easy to reduce to a single striking image. Walking the same coastline where diving actually happens, alongside a museum that provides historical and cultural context, gives visitors a fuller and more respectful understanding of the tradition — including its physical demands and its declining numbers — rather than a one-dimensional impression.

## Local Experience Angle

Because haenyeo are working divers, not performers, this experience is framed around observation and learning rather than staged interaction. Visiting in the morning increases the chance of seeing divers active in the water, though this cannot be guaranteed and should not be the sole reason for visiting — the museum and coastal walk carry the experience on their own.
