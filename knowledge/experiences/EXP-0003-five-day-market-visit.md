# Guided Visit to a Jeju Five-Day Market

## Summary

This experience is a slower, more intentional way to visit one of Jeju's five-day markets (owe-il-jang) than simply passing through — arriving early, walking the produce and seafood stalls with an eye for what is in season, sampling prepared food, and talking with vendors about what they are selling and why. It treats the market as a destination in its own right, not a stop on the way to somewhere else.

## Why It Matters

The five-day market system (see `LOCAL-0001`) is one of the clearest windows into how Jeju residents actually shop, eat, and interact day to day. A guided or intentional visit helps travelers read a market that can otherwise feel chaotic or opaque to an outsider — understanding the rotation schedule, recognizing seasonal produce, and knowing where prepared-food stalls like bingtteok vendors (see `FOOD-0001`) tend to cluster.

## Local Experience Angle

Unlike a curated food tour with fixed stops, a market visit rewards wandering and asking questions. Arriving early gives the best selection, particularly of fresh seafood, and visitors who are willing to sample unfamiliar prepared foods — not just photograph them — get the most out of the experience. Cash is commonly preferred by smaller vendors.
