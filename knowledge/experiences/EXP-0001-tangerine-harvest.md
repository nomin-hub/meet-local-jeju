# Hands-On Tangerine Harvesting in Seogwipo

## Summary

During Jeju's winter harvest season, family-run citrus farms around Seogwipo open their groves to visitors for hands-on tangerine (gamgyul) picking. Rather than buying pre-packaged fruit, visitors pick directly from the trees, often guided informally by the farmers themselves, and can typically eat as they pick and take home a portion of the harvest.

## Why It Matters

This experience turns an abstract idea — "Jeju is known for tangerines" — into a direct, physical understanding of the seasonal agricultural labor that underpins much of the island's rural economy (see `STORY-0002`). It connects visitors to a working farm and a working family, not a staged photo backdrop.

## Local Experience Angle

Sessions tend to be informal and unhurried: a short orientation on identifying ripe fruit and picking without damaging the tree, followed by open picking time, with conversation alongside the farming family as a natural part of the visit. This stands in contrast to more commercialized, ticketed "photo-op" orchard attractions — the emphasis here is on participating in real seasonal work, not consuming a curated experience of it.
