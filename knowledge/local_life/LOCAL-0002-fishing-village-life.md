# Daily Rhythms of a Jeju Fishing Village

## Summary

In coastal villages like Gimnyeong-ri, the day often begins at the small harbor before sunrise, as fishing boats head out and haenyeo (see `STORY-0001`) gather for the morning's diving. By mid-morning, the catch and the day's harvest from the sea move toward local buyers, household kitchens, and nearby markets, while the rest of the village settles into a slower daily pace — small shops opening, boats being maintained, nets repaired along the harbor wall.

## Why It Matters

Fishing villages are where several of JEJU-KB's categories intersect in lived practice rather than in the abstract: haenyeo diving, seafood-based food culture, and the low stone walls (see `CULTURE-0001`) that shelter homes and small fields from Jeju's coastal winds. Understanding a fishing village's daily rhythm gives travelers a frame for reading what they see when they pass through one, rather than experiencing it as generic scenery.

## Local Experience Angle

Fishing villages reward slow, observational visits more than scheduled activities — walking the harbor in the early morning, watching the rhythm of boats and divers, and noticing how compact and interdependent daily life is in a small coastal community. This is less a single "thing to do" than a way of moving through a place attentively.
