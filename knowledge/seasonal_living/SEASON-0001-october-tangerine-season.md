# October in the Orchards: The Start of Jeju's Tangerine Season

## Summary

October marks the early edge of Jeju's tangerine (gamgyul) season, as fruit that has grown through the summer begins turning from green to orange and certain early-ripening varieties start to be lightly harvested. The bulk of the harvest — the busiest and most physically demanding stretch — comes later, through the winter months (see `EXP-0001`), but October is when the orchards visibly signal that the season has begun.

## Why It Matters

Seasonal awareness is central to giving travelers accurate, non-generic recommendations. A visitor asking about tangerines in October should be told they are seeing the start of the season, not the peak harvest — a distinction that changes what kind of experience is realistic to expect (light color-change viewing and early picking, rather than full harvest participation).

## Local Experience Angle

For travelers on Jeju in October specifically, this is a good window for orchard walks and observing the color change across the hillsides around Seogwipo, even if full hands-on harvesting opportunities are more limited than they will be later in the season. It also sets up anticipation for a return visit, or for travelers with flexible winter plans, timing a harvest experience for its peak.
